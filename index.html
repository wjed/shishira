Local Writer & Marketing Student Makes Waves at JMU

Shishira Nakka combines literary passion with strategic communication expertise

In the bustling halls of James Madison University, one student stands out for her unique ability to bridge the worlds of creative writing and strategic marketing. Shishira Nakka, a dual-degree student pursuing both Marketing (BBA) and English Language and Literature (BA), has been making significant contributions to campus publications and local organizations since beginning her academic journey.

Currently serving as Social Media Manager for WXJM 88.7 FM and Contributing Columnist for The Breeze, Nakka has demonstrated an exceptional talent for authentic storytelling that resonates with diverse audiences. "I love telling stories that actually matter," she explains. "Whether I'm writing for The Breeze, creating content for brands, or working on marketing campaigns, I'm always looking for ways to connect with people through honest, engaging writing."

Her recent opinion piece, "Not a fan of the fake tan," published in The Breeze in February 2025, exemplifies her approach to tackling complex social issues with both humor and insight. The article explores authenticity and self-expression in college culture, challenging readers to embrace their natural selves in a world obsessed with artificial perfection.

The Art of Authentic Storytelling

Writer's philosophy centers on connection and genuine expression

Nakka's approach to writing is deeply rooted in authenticity and human connection. "Every story has the power to change someone's perspective, spark a conversation, or simply make someone feel less alone in their experience," she explains.

Her writing spans multiple categories, from opinion pieces and personal essays to strategic marketing copy. Whether crafting content for social media, developing PR strategies, or contributing to campus publications, she consistently seeks to find the human story within every narrative.

This philosophy was evident in her personal essay "A Letter to My Younger Self," featured in Unfiltered Magazine's fourth issue and shared with over 10,000 followers. The piece offered a deeply personal reflection on growth, resilience, and the wisdom that comes with experience.
