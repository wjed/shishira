Hi, I'm Shishira — and I write things.

I study Marketing and English at JMU, and most of my days involve trying to say something true.

I'm a sophomore at James Madison University double-majoring in Marketing (BBA) and English Language and Literature (BA). My days usually involve writing — whether that's an opinion column for The Breeze, a feature story for the JMU College of Integrated Science and Engineering, content for WXJM 88.7's social channels, or a Word doc I'll never show anyone.

I also host a radio show called Death by Chocolate — every Wednesday, 4–5:30pm, 88.7 on your FM dial. It's about the sweet layers of life. I love it more than I can explain.

My hand is permanently scarred where I hold my wooden pencil, and there are fingerprints all over my laptop screen from showing people what I'm working on. I chase the chance to create, in a professional sense and an artistic one. Doing my best.

My opinion piece "Not a fan of the fake tan," published in The Breeze in February 2025, is a good example of how I write — with humor, honesty, and a real point of view. I care about writing that makes people feel something, even if that something is just "huh, same."

Why I write the way I write

It's about connection, not performance

I believe every story has the power to change someone's perspective, spark a conversation, or make someone feel less alone. That's the standard I hold myself to, whether I'm writing an opinion piece, a marketing campaign, or a personal essay.

Writing spans everything I do — social media, PR strategy, news features, opinions, creative pieces. The format changes but the goal is the same: find the real story and tell it honestly.

In 2023, my personal essay "A Letter to My Younger Self" was published in Unfiltered Magazine's fourth issue and featured on their Instagram to over 10,000 followers. That felt good. I want to keep making things that feel like that.

Things I actually believe

The stuff that shapes how I work and why I show up

Authenticity

I write what's true. I don't perform enthusiasm I don't have, and I don't flatten a story to make it easier to tell. Genuine always beats polished.

Collaboration

The best work I've done has been with other people. Diverse perspectives make everything sharper — I've seen it firsthand in consulting, in the newsroom, and on radio.

Growth

I'm a sophomore who doesn't know everything, and I'm okay with that. I'd rather ask questions and keep learning than pretend I've got it figured out.

Care

Every piece I write, every project I'm part of — I want it to be something I'm proud of. Not perfect, but real and done with intention.
